Inventing the Future of Space Travel

Bold and bright engineers are dreaming of the first affordable technology to get into space. The current level of technology for launching spacecraft to orbit would have astounded earlier generations, and what the future holds is greater than fiction.

Jules Vernes imagined sending a handful of men to the Moon by guncotton in the 1800s. Today's engineers are imagining systems that can be used to mine and colonize the solar system, by transporting anyone to orbit and beyond for an affordable price.

Get a Vision of the Future

Various scientists from all over the world want to share their dreams and designs for space travel at the LEO Expo. They're showcasing their work to help inspire the planet and win the Expo prize--a research grant--to fund their dreams.

At the Expo, you can meet, listen, and see their visions in a world-class indoor exposition. Each team has their own booths and virtual demonstrations of the technology they are developing.
